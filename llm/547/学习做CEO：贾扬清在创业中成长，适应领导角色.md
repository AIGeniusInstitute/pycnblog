#### 4.1 成本效益分析（Cost-Benefit Analysis）

成本效益分析是一种常用的评估决策有效性的方法，它通过计算项目的成本和预期收益，帮助决策者确定是否实施某个项目。以下是一个简单的成本效益分析公式：

\[ CBA = \frac{B}{C} \]

其中，\( B \) 表示预期收益，\( C \) 表示总成本。

**举例说明**：

假设旷视科技正在考虑是否投资一个新的人工智能项目。根据市场调研，该项目预计带来1000万元的收益，而总成本为500万元。那么，成本效益分析如下：

\[ CBA = \frac{1000}{500} = 2 \]

成本效益比为2，这意味着每投入1元，预计能获得2元的收益。根据这一分析，旷视科技决定投资该项目。

#### 4.2 技术评估矩阵（Technology Evaluation Matrix）

技术评估矩阵是一种用于评估不同技术可行性和风险的工具。以下是一个简单的技术评估矩阵公式：

\[ TEMA = \begin{pmatrix}
F_1 & R_1 \\
F_2 & R_2 \\
\vdots & \vdots \\
F_n & R_n
\end{pmatrix} \]

其中，\( F_i \) 表示技术的可行性，\( R_i \) 表示风险。

**举例说明**：

旷视科技正在评估两种不同的人工智能技术A和B。根据评估，技术A的可行性为0.8，风险为0.2；技术B的可行性为0.7，风险为0.3。那么，技术评估矩阵如下：

\[ TEMA = \begin{pmatrix}
0.8 & 0.2 \\
0.7 & 0.3
\end{pmatrix} \]

根据技术评估矩阵，旷视科技可以选择技术A，因为它具有较高的可行性和较低的风险。

#### 4.3 360度绩效评估（360-Degree Performance Evaluation）

360度绩效评估是一种收集多方反馈的方法，用于全面评估员工的绩效。以下是一个简单的360度绩效评估公式：

\[ PE = \frac{S + C + U + O}{4} \]

其中，\( S \) 表示上级评价，\( C \) 表示同事评价，\( U \) 表示下属评价，\( O \) 表示自我评价。

**举例说明**：

假设某员工的360度绩效评估结果如下：上级评价为90分，同事评价为85分，下属评价为80分，自我评价为75分。那么，绩效评估结果为：

\[ PE = \frac{90 + 85 + 80 + 75}{4} = 83.75 \]

这个分数表明该员工的整体绩效表现较好。

#### 4.4 敏捷开发方法（Agile Development）

敏捷开发是一种通过迭代和持续反馈来提高团队协作效率和项目成功率的开发方法。以下是一个简单的敏捷开发流程：

1. **需求分析**：收集用户需求和业务目标。
2. **迭代规划**：确定每个迭代的目标和任务。
3. **开发与测试**：在迭代周期内完成开发任务并进行测试。
4. **评审与反馈**：评审迭代成果并收集反馈，为下一个迭代做好准备。

#### 4.1 Cost-Benefit Analysis

Cost-benefit analysis is a commonly used method to assess the effectiveness of decisions by calculating the cost and expected benefits of a project. The following is a simple cost-benefit analysis formula:

\[ CBA = \frac{B}{C} \]

where \( B \) represents the expected benefits and \( C \) represents the total cost.

**Example Illustration**:

Suppose M egvii Technology is considering whether to invest in a new artificial intelligence project. According to market research, the project is expected to generate 10 million yuan in benefits with a total cost of 5 million yuan. The cost-benefit analysis would be as follows:

\[ CBA = \frac{10}{5} = 2 \]

The cost-benefit ratio is 2, meaning that for every 1 yuan invested, an expected return of 2 yuan is anticipated. Based on this analysis, Megvii Technology decides to invest in the project.

#### 4.2 Technology Evaluation Matrix

A technology evaluation matrix is a tool used to assess the feasibility and risks of different technologies. The following is a simple technology evaluation matrix formula:

\[ TEMA = \begin{pmatrix}
F_1 & R_1 \\
F_2 & R_2 \\
\vdots & \vdots \\
F_n & R_n
\end{pmatrix} \]

where \( F_i \) represents the feasibility of the technology and \( R_i \) represents the risk.

**Example Illustration**:

Suppose Megvii Technology is evaluating two different artificial intelligence technologies, A and B. According to the evaluation, technology A has a feasibility of 0.8 and a risk of 0.2, while technology B has a feasibility of 0.7 and a risk of 0.3. The technology evaluation matrix would be as follows:

\[ TEMA = \begin{pmatrix}
0.8 & 0.2 \\
0.7 & 0.3
\end{pmatrix} \]

Based on the technology evaluation matrix, Megvii Technology would choose technology A, as it has higher feasibility and lower risk.

#### 4.3 360-Degree Performance Evaluation

A 360-degree performance evaluation is a method of collecting feedback from multiple parties to comprehensively assess an employee's performance. The following is a simple 360-degree performance evaluation formula:

\[ PE = \frac{S + C + U + O}{4} \]

where \( S \) represents supervisor evaluation, \( C \) represents peer evaluation, \( U \) represents subordinate evaluation, and \( O \) represents self-evaluation.

**Example Illustration**:

Suppose an employee's 360-degree performance evaluation results are as follows: supervisor evaluation of 90 points, peer evaluation of 85 points, subordinate evaluation of 80 points, and self-evaluation of 75 points. The performance evaluation result would be:

\[ PE = \frac{90 + 85 + 80 + 75}{4} = 83.75 \]

This score indicates that the employee has overall good performance.
