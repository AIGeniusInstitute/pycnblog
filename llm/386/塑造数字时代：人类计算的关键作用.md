#### 3.1 人类计算的核心算法原理

3. **结果整合**：将各个子任务的结果整合起来，得到最终的任务结果。

在人类计算中，数学模型和公式扮演着至关重要的角色。它们不仅用于任务分解和结果整合，还用于评估任务完成的质量和效率。以下是一些常见的数学模型和公式，以及它们的详细解释和举例说明。

#### 4.1 熵（Entropy）

熵是衡量信息不确定性的度量，它在人类计算中用于评估任务分解的有效性。熵的计算公式如下：

$$
H(X) = -\sum_{i} p(x_i) \log_2 p(x_i)
$$

其中，\(X\) 表示随机变量，\(p(x_i)\) 表示 \(x_i\) 发生的概率。

**例子**：假设我们有一个图像识别任务，其中图像可以分为5个类别。如果我们随机选择一个图像，那么每个类别发生的概率相等，即 \(p(x_i) = 0.2\)。使用熵公式计算，我们得到：

$$
H(X) = -5 \times 0.2 \log_2 0.2 \approx 1.89
$$

这表明，随机选择一个图像时，我们对其类别的不确定性较高。

#### 4.2 条件熵（Conditional Entropy）

条件熵是衡量给定一个随机变量后，另一个随机变量的不确定性。它在人类计算中用于评估任务分解后的子任务质量。条件熵的计算公式如下：

$$
H(X|Y) = -\sum_{i} p(x_i|y_i) \log_2 p(x_i|y_i)
$$

其中，\(X\) 和 \(Y\) 分别表示两个随机变量，\(p(x_i|y_i)\) 表示在 \(Y=y_i\) 条件下 \(X=x_i\) 的概率。

**例子**：假设我们有一个图像识别任务，其中图像可以分为5个类别，每个类别有10个图像。如果我们已知图像的类别，那么条件熵可以用来衡量图像特征提取的质量。假设某个类别下的特征提取结果不确定性较低，我们可以通过条件熵来评估。

#### 4.3 信息增益（Information Gain）

信息增益是衡量一个特征对分类的贡献。它在人类计算中用于评估子任务的分配质量。信息增益的计算公式如下：

$$
I(X; Y) = H(X) - H(X|Y)
$$

其中，\(X\) 和 \(Y\) 分别表示特征和类别，\(H(X)\) 和 \(H(X|Y)\) 分别表示特征和条件熵。

**例子**：假设我们有一个图像识别任务，其中图像可以分为5个类别，每个类别有10个图像。如果我们使用一个特征来区分这些类别，信息增益可以用来衡量这个特征对分类的贡献。例如，如果我们发现某个特征使得类别之间的条件熵较低，那么这个特征对分类的贡献较大。

#### 4.4 决策树（Decision Tree）

决策树是一种常见的分类算法，它在人类计算中用于任务分解和结果整合。决策树的基本原理是根据特征选择最佳分割点，将数据集划分成多个子集，并递归地应用这个原则，直到满足停止条件。

**例子**：假设我们有一个图像识别任务，其中图像可以分为5个类别。我们可以使用决策树来分解任务，将图像分成多个子任务，例如根据颜色、形状和纹理等特征。通过递归地划分数据集，我们可以得到多个子任务，从而提高任务完成的质量和效率。

### Mathematical Models and Formulas & Detailed Explanation & Examples

In human computation, mathematical models and formulas play a crucial role. They are not only used for task decomposition and result integration but also for evaluating the quality and efficiency of task completion. Here are some common mathematical models and formulas, along with their detailed explanations and examples.

#### 4.1 Entropy

Entropy is a measure of information uncertainty and is used in human computation to evaluate the effectiveness of task decomposition. The formula for entropy is:

$$
H(X) = -\sum_{i} p(x_i) \log_2 p(x_i)
$$

where \(X\) is a random variable and \(p(x_i)\) is the probability of \(x_i\) occurring.

**Example**: Suppose we have an image recognition task where images can be classified into 5 categories. If we randomly select an image, the probability of each category is equal, i.e., \(p(x_i) = 0.2\). Using the entropy formula, we get:

$$
H(X) = -5 \times 0.2 \log_2 0.2 \approx 1.89
$$

This indicates that when randomly selecting an image, we have a high level of uncertainty about its category.

#### 4.2 Conditional Entropy

Conditional entropy measures the uncertainty of one random variable given another and is used in human computation to evaluate the quality of subtasks after decomposition. The formula for conditional entropy is:

$$
H(X|Y) = -\sum_{i} p(x_i|y_i) \log_2 p(x_i|y_i)
$$

where \(X\) and \(Y\) are two random variables, and \(p(x_i|y_i)\) is the probability of \(x_i\) occurring given \(Y = y_i\).

**Example**: Suppose we have an image recognition task where images can be classified into 5 categories, and each category has 10 images. Conditional entropy can be used to evaluate the quality of feature extraction. For instance, if we find that the conditional entropy of a certain category is low, it indicates that the feature extraction for that category is of high quality.

#### 4.3 Information Gain

Information gain measures the contribution of a feature to classification and is used in human computation to evaluate the quality of subtask allocation. The formula for information gain is:

$$
I(X; Y) = H(X) - H(X|Y)
$$

where \(X\) and \(Y\) are the feature and category, and \(H(X)\) and \(H(X|Y)\) are the entropy and conditional entropy, respectively.

**Example**: Suppose we have an image recognition task where images can be classified into 5 categories, and each category has 10 images. If we use a feature to distinguish these categories, information gain can be used to evaluate the contribution of this feature to classification. For instance, if we find that the information gain of a certain feature is high, it indicates that this feature is highly valuable for classification.

#### 4.4 Decision Tree

A decision tree is a common classification algorithm used in human computation for task decomposition and result integration. The basic principle of a decision tree is to select the best split point based on a feature to divide the dataset into multiple subsets and recursively apply this principle until a stopping condition is met.

**Example**: Suppose we have an image recognition task where images can be classified into 5 categories. We can use a decision tree to decompose the task by dividing images into multiple subtasks based on features such as color, shape, and texture. By recursively dividing the dataset, we can obtain multiple subtasks, thereby improving the quality and efficiency of task completion.
