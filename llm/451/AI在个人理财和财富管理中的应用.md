<|user|>### 4. 数学模型和公式 & 详细讲解 & 举例说明

#### 4.1 投资组合优化中的数学模型

在投资组合优化中，常见的数学模型包括均值-方差模型和资本资产定价模型（CAPM）。

**均值-方差模型**：

均值-方差模型是一种基于预期收益率和风险（方差）的投资组合优化模型。其核心公式为：

$$
\text{Maximize} \ \ \mathbb{E}[R_p] - \lambda \text{Var}(R_p)
$$

其中，$\mathbb{E}[R_p]$表示投资组合的预期收益率，$\text{Var}(R_p)$表示投资组合的风险（方差），$\lambda$是一个权重系数，用于平衡收益和风险。

**资本资产定价模型（CAPM）**：

资本资产定价模型是一种用于评估资产定价和风险收益关系的模型。其核心公式为：

$$
\text{Expected Return} = \text{Risk-Free Rate} + \beta \times \text{Market Risk Premium}
$$

其中，$\text{Risk-Free Rate}$表示无风险收益率，$\beta$表示资产的风险系数，$\text{Market Risk Premium}$表示市场风险溢价。

#### 4.2 数学模型的应用示例

**示例 1：均值-方差模型的优化**

假设有两个投资选项A和B，其预期收益率和风险如下表所示：

| 投资选项 | 预期收益率 | 风险 |
| :------: | :--------: | :--: |
|   A     |   10%     |  20% |
|   B     |   15%     |  30% |

我们需要找到一个最优的投资组合，使其预期收益率最大，风险最小。

首先，计算两个投资选项的权重：

$$
w_A = \frac{\text{Expected Return of Portfolio} - \text{Expected Return of Asset B}}{\text{Expected Return of Asset A} - \text{Expected Return of Asset B}}
$$

$$
w_B = 1 - w_A
$$

然后，根据权重计算投资组合的预期收益率和风险：

$$
\text{Expected Return of Portfolio} = w_A \times \text{Expected Return of Asset A} + w_B \times \text{Expected Return of Asset B}
$$

$$
\text{Var}(R_p) = w_A^2 \times \text{Var}(R_A) + w_B^2 \times \text{Var}(R_B) + 2w_Aw_B \text{Cov}(R_A, R_B)
$$

通过优化目标函数，我们可以找到一个最优的投资组合，使其预期收益率最大，风险最小。

**示例 2：CAPM的应用**

假设市场风险溢价为8%，无风险收益率为3%，某资产的贝塔系数为1.5，我们需要计算该资产的预期收益率。

根据CAPM公式：

$$
\text{Expected Return} = 3\% + 1.5 \times 8\% = 15\%
$$

这意味着该资产的预期收益率为15%。

### 4. Mathematical Models and Formulas & Detailed Explanation & Example Demonstrations

#### 4.1 Mathematical Models in Portfolio Optimization

In portfolio optimization, common mathematical models include the mean-variance model and the Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM).

**Mean-Variance Model**:

The mean-variance model is an investment portfolio optimization model based on expected return and risk (variance). Its core formula is:

$$
\text{Maximize} \ \ \mathbb{E}[R_p] - \lambda \text{Var}(R_p)
$$

Where $\mathbb{E}[R_p]$ represents the expected return of the investment portfolio, $\text{Var}(R_p)$ represents the risk (variance) of the investment portfolio, and $\lambda$ is a weight coefficient used to balance returns and risk.

**Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM)**:

The CAPM is a model used to evaluate the pricing and risk-return relationship of assets. Its core formula is:

$$
\text{Expected Return} = \text{Risk-Free Rate} + \beta \times \text{Market Risk Premium}
$$

Where $\text{Risk-Free Rate}$ represents the risk-free return, $\beta$ represents the asset's risk coefficient, and $\text{Market Risk Premium}$ represents the market risk premium.

#### 4.2 Application of Mathematical Models

**Example 1: Optimization of the Mean-Variance Model**

Assuming there are two investment options A and B, with the following expected returns and risks:

| Investment Option | Expected Return | Risk |
| :---------------: | :-------------: | :--: |
|        A         |       10%      |  20% |
|        B         |       15%      |  30% |

We need to find an optimal investment portfolio that maximizes the expected return and minimizes the risk.

First, calculate the weights of the two investment options:

$$
w_A = \frac{\text{Expected Return of Portfolio} - \text{Expected Return of Asset B}}{\text{Expected Return of Asset A} - \text{Expected Return of Asset B}}
$$

$$
w_B = 1 - w_A
$$

Then, calculate the expected return and risk of the investment portfolio based on the weights:

$$
\text{Expected Return of Portfolio} = w_A \times \text{Expected Return of Asset A} + w_B \times \text{Expected Return of Asset B}
$$

$$
\text{Var}(R_p) = w_A^2 \times \text{Var}(R_A) + w_B^2 \times \text{Var}(R_B) + 2w_Aw_B \text{Cov}(R_A, R_B)
$$

By optimizing the objective function, we can find an optimal investment portfolio that maximizes the expected return and minimizes the risk.

**Example 2: Application of CAPM**

Assuming the market risk premium is 8% and the risk-free rate is 3%, and the beta coefficient of an asset is 1.5, we need to calculate the expected return of this asset.

According to the CAPM formula:

$$
\text{Expected Return} = 3\% + 1.5 \times 8\% = 15\%
$$

This means the expected return of the asset is 15%.
