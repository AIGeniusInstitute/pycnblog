#### 4.1 线性规划（Linear Programming）

线性规划是一种用于在给定约束条件下最大化或最小化线性函数的数学方法。其标准形式如下：

\[ \text{Maximize} \quad c^T x \]
\[ \text{Subject to} \quad Ax \leq b \]
\[ x \geq 0 \]

其中，\( c \) 是目标函数的系数向量，\( x \) 是决策变量向量，\( A \) 是约束条件的系数矩阵，\( b \) 是约束条件的常数向量。

**例子**：一家制造公司生产两种产品 A 和 B，每种产品都有一定的生产成本和利润。公司希望最大化总利润，同时满足原材料和劳动力的限制。

假设生产产品 A 的成本为 2 单位，利润为 5 单位；生产产品 B 的成本为 3 单位，利润为 7 单位。现有 10 单位的原材料和 20 单位的劳动力。

目标函数：最大化总利润 \( Z = 5x + 7y \)

约束条件：
\[ 2x + 3y \leq 10 \]（原材料限制）
\[ x + 2y \leq 20 \]（劳动力限制）

通过求解线性规划问题，公司可以确定最优的生产方案，从而最大化总利润。

#### 4.3 马尔可夫链（Markov Chain）

马尔可夫链是一种用于描述随机过程的数学模型。它通过状态转移矩阵描述系统在不同状态之间的转移概率。

**例子**：一个城市中居民的生活水平可以分为三个等级：富裕、中产和贫困。根据历史数据，我们可以构建一个马尔可夫链来预测未来居民生活水平的变化。

假设状态转移矩阵 \( P \) 如下：

\[ P = \begin{bmatrix} 
0.2 & 0.6 & 0.2 \\ 
0.1 & 0.7 & 0.2 \\ 
0.3 & 0.4 & 0.3 
\end{bmatrix} \]

表示当前状态为富裕、中产和贫困的概率分别为 0.2、0.6 和 0.2，下一个状态为富裕、中产和贫困的概率分别为 0.1、0.7 和 0.2。

给定当前状态分布 \( \pi \)，可以计算未来状态的分布：

\[ \pi_{t+1} = P \pi_t \]

通过迭代计算，我们可以预测未来居民生活水平的变化趋势，为政策制定提供参考。

#### 4.4 线性回归（Linear Regression）

线性回归是一种用于建立自变量和因变量之间线性关系的数学模型。其公式如下：

\[ y = \beta_0 + \beta_1 x + \epsilon \]

其中，\( y \) 是因变量，\( x \) 是自变量，\( \beta_0 \) 和 \( \beta_1 \) 是模型参数，\( \epsilon \) 是误差项。

**例子**：一个房地产分析师希望建立房价与面积之间的关系模型。

给定数据如下：

\[ \begin{array}{cc} 
x (面积) & y (房价) \\ 
1000 & 200000 \\ 
1500 & 300000 \\ 
2000 & 400000 \\ 
2500 & 500000 
\end{array} \]

通过最小二乘法求解线性回归模型，可以得到：

\[ y = 100000 + 200000 \times x \]

这个模型可以用于预测不同面积房屋的房价，为房地产投资提供参考。

#### 4.1 Linear Programming

Linear programming is a mathematical method used to maximize or minimize a linear function under given constraints. The standard form of a linear programming problem is as follows:

\[ \text{Maximize} \quad c^T x \]
\[ \text{Subject to} \quad Ax \leq b \]
\[ x \geq 0 \]

Where \( c \) is the coefficient vector of the objective function, \( x \) is the decision variable vector, \( A \) is the coefficient matrix of the constraints, and \( b \) is the constant vector of the constraints.

**Example**: A manufacturing company produces two products, A and B, each with certain production costs and profits. The company aims to maximize total profit while satisfying limitations on raw materials and labor.

Assuming the production cost of product A is 2 units and the profit is 5 units; the production cost of product B is 3 units and the profit is 7 units. There are 10 units of raw materials and 20 units of labor available.

Objective function: Maximize total profit \( Z = 5x + 7y \)

Constraints:
\[ 2x + 3y \leq 10 \] (raw material constraint)
\[ x + 2y \leq 20 \] (labor constraint)

By solving the linear programming problem, the company can determine the optimal production plan to maximize total profit.

#### 4.2 Decision Trees

A decision tree is a graphical model used for classification and regression analysis. It divides a dataset into different categories or predicted values through a series of decision nodes and leaf nodes.

#### 4.3 Markov Chains

A Markov chain is a mathematical model used to describe stochastic processes. It represents the transition probabilities between different states of a system through a transition matrix.

**Example**: In a city, the living standards of residents can be categorized into three levels: affluent, middle-class, and poor. Based on historical data, we can construct a Markov chain to predict the changes in residents' living standards in the future.

Assuming the state transition matrix \( P \) is as follows:

\[ P = \begin{bmatrix} 
0.2 & 0.6 & 0.2 \\ 
0.1 & 0.7 & 0.2 \\ 
0.3 & 0.4 & 0.3 
\end{bmatrix} \]

This indicates that the probabilities of being in the affluent, middle-class, and poor states are 0.2, 0.6, and 0.2, respectively, and the probabilities of transitioning to the affluent, middle-class, and poor states are 0.1, 0.7, and 0.2, respectively.

Given the current state distribution \( \pi \), we can calculate the future state distribution:

\[ \pi_{t+1} = P \pi_t \]

Through iterative calculation, we can predict the trend of changes in residents' living standards, providing a reference for policy-making.

#### 4.4 Linear Regression

Linear regression is a mathematical model used to establish a linear relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables. The formula is as follows:

\[ y = \beta_0 + \beta_1 x + \epsilon \]

Where \( y \) is the dependent variable, \( x \) is the independent variable, \( \beta_0 \) and \( \beta_1 \) are the model parameters, and \( \epsilon \) is the error term.

**Example**: A real estate analyst wants to establish a model to predict the relationship between house price and area.

Given the following data:

\[ \begin{array}{cc} 
x (\text{Area}) & y (\text{House Price}) \\ 
1000 & 200,000 \\ 
1500 & 300,000 \\ 
2000 & 400,000 \\ 
2500 & 500,000 
\end{array} \]

By solving the linear regression model using the least squares method, we can obtain:

\[ y = 100,000 + 200,000 \times x \]

This model can be used to predict the price of houses with different areas, providing a reference for real estate investment.
