### 1.2 Concepts of Carbon Neutrality and Negative Carbon Economy

Carbon neutrality refers to the achievement of net-zero greenhouse gas emissions through a combination of reducing emissions, capturing and storing carbon, and offsetting residual emissions. Specifically, carbon neutrality involves the following aspects:

1. **Reduction of emissions**: Through increased energy efficiency, the use of renewable energy sources, and optimization of industrial processes to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
2. **Carbon capture**: The use of technologies to capture and store carbon dioxide emissions from industrial processes.
3. **Carbon offsetting**: The purchase of carbon credits or participation in projects such as reforestation to compensate for emissions that cannot be reduced immediately.

Negative carbon economy takes carbon neutrality a step further by not only achieving net-zero emissions but also removing additional carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, thus achieving negative emissions. Key technologies for negative carbon economy include carbon capture and storage (CCS) and biomass energy with carbon capture and storage (BECCS).

### 1.3 Background of Global Emission Reduction Targets by 2050

To address the challenge of climate change, the international community reached the Paris Agreement in 2015, which outlines national targets and actions for each country. The agreement aims to limit the global temperature increase to 2 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels, with efforts to limit it to 1.5 degrees Celsius. To achieve this goal, the global community needs to achieve carbon neutrality by 2050, and even transition to a negative carbon economy.

### 1.4 Importance of Technological Innovation and Policy Regulations

Technological innovation includes the development of more efficient energy technologies, the improvement of industrial processes and materials, and the enhancement of energy storage capabilities. Policy regulations, on the other hand, use mechanisms such as carbon pricing, the promotion of renewable energy, strengthened environmental regulations, and the implementation of international carbon trading systems to drive global emission reduction efforts.
