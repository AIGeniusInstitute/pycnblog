### Mathematical Models and Formulas & Detailed Explanation & Examples

To design an effective knowledge payment-based online skills competition and event organization service, it is essential to understand and apply several mathematical models and formulas. These models can help in optimizing pricing strategies, predicting user behavior, and analyzing the performance of the system. Below, we discuss some of the key mathematical concepts and provide detailed explanations along with examples.

#### 1. Cost-Benefit Analysis

**Formula:** \( \text{Net Profit} = (\text{Revenue} - \text{Cost}) \times \text{Profit Margin} \)

**Explanation:** This formula represents the net profit derived from a knowledge payment-based service. It considers the total revenue generated from user subscriptions and competition entry fees, minus the costs associated with content creation, platform maintenance, and marketing expenses. The profit margin is the percentage of revenue that remains after these costs are deducted.

**Example:** Suppose a service charges $10 per month for a basic subscription and $30 per month for a premium subscription. The total monthly revenue is calculated as \( (100 \times \$10) + (50 \times \$30) = \$400 + \$1500 = \$2500 \). If the total monthly cost is \$1000, the net profit would be \( \$2500 - \$1000 = \$1500 \).

#### 2. Pricing Optimization

**Formula:** \( \text{Optimal Price} = \frac{\text{Average Cost}}{1 - \text{Marginal Revenue}} \)

**Explanation:** This formula helps determine the optimal price for a subscription or course based on the average cost and marginal revenue. Marginal revenue is the additional revenue generated by selling one more unit of the product. The optimal price maximizes profit by balancing cost and revenue.

**Example:** If the average cost per user is \$15 and the marginal revenue is \$5, the optimal price would be \( \$15 / (1 - \$5) = \$15 / (-\$4) = \$3.75 \). This means that to maximize profit, the service should charge \$3.75 per month.

#### 3. Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

**Formula:** \( \text{CLV} = \frac{\text{Average Revenue per User}}{\text{Churn Rate}} \)

**Explanation:** The Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) is a predictive metric that estimates the total revenue a user is expected to generate over their entire relationship with the service. It is calculated by dividing the average revenue per user by the churn rate, which represents the percentage of users who cancel their subscriptions within a given period.

**Example:** If the average revenue per user is \$50 and the churn rate is 10%, the CLV would be \( \$50 / 0.10 = \$500 \). This indicates that, on average, a user is expected to contribute \$500 in revenue over their entire subscription period.

#### 4. Conversion Rate Optimization

**Formula:** \( \text{Conversion Rate} = \frac{\text{Number of Conversions}}{\text{Total Visitors}} \)

**Explanation:** The conversion rate is a measure of the effectiveness of a pricing strategy or marketing campaign in converting visitors into paying customers. It is calculated by dividing the number of conversions (e.g., sign-ups or purchases) by the total number of visitors.

**Example:** If 1000 visitors resulted in 200 sign-ups, the conversion rate would be \( 200 / 1000 = 0.20 \) or 20%.

#### 5. Resource Allocation

**Formula:** \( \text{Optimal Resource Allocation} = \frac{\text{Total Resource Budget}}{\text{Number of Projects}} \)

**Explanation:** This formula helps allocate resources efficiently among multiple projects or initiatives. It divides the total resource budget by the number of projects to determine the optimal amount of resources to allocate to each project.

**Example:** If a company has a total resource budget of \$100,000 and three projects, the optimal resource allocation would be \( \$100,000 / 3 = \$33,333 \) per project.

By applying these mathematical models and formulas, organizations can make informed decisions about pricing strategies, resource allocation, and customer engagement, ultimately enhancing the effectiveness and profitability of their knowledge payment-based online skills competition and event organization services.

from flask import Flask, request, jsonify
from werkzeug.security import generate_password_hash, check_password_hash

app = Flask(__name__)
app.config['SQLALCHEMY_DATABASE_URI'] = 'postgresql://username:password@localhost/db_name'
db.init_app(app)

@app.route('/register', methods=['POST'])
def register():
    data = request.get_json()
    username = data.get('username')
    email = data.get('email')
    password = data.get('password')
    
    if not username or not email or not password:
        return jsonify({'error': 'Missing required fields'}), 400
    
    user = User.query.filter_by(username=username).first()
    if user:
        return jsonify({'error': 'Username already exists'}), 409
    
    new_user = User(username=username, email=email, password_hash=generate_password_hash(password))
    db.session.add(new_user)
    db.session.commit()
    
    return jsonify({'message': 'User registered successfully'}), 201

@app.route('/login', methods=['POST'])
def login():
    data = request.get_json()
    username = data.get('username')
    password = data.get('password')
    
    if not username or not password:
        return jsonify({'error': 'Missing required fields'}), 400
    
    user = User.query.filter_by(username=username).first()
    if not user or not user.check_password(password):
        return jsonify({'error': 'Invalid credentials'}), 401
    
    return jsonify({'message': 'Login successful'}), 200

@app.route('/courses', methods=['GET'])
def get_courses():
    courses = Course.query.all()
    return jsonify([{'id': course.id, 'name': course.name, 'description': course.description, 'price': course.price} for course in courses])

@app.route('/competitions', methods=['GET'])
def get_competitions():
    competitions = Competition.query.all()
    return jsonify([{'id': competition.id, 'name': competition.name, 'description': competition.description, 'start_date': competition.start_date, 'end_date': competition.end_date, 'entry_fee': competition.entry_fee} for competition in competitions])

if __name__ == '__main__':
    app.run(debug=True)
