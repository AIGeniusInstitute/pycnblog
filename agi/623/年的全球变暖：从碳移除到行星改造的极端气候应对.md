##### 3.1 碳移除技术的核心算法原理

**直接空气捕捉（DAC）**：DAC技术的核心算法涉及利用吸附剂或吸收剂将二氧化碳从空气中分离出来。常用的吸附剂包括胺类化合物、有机溶剂和金属有机框架（MOFs）。以下是DAC技术的基本步骤：

1. **进气处理**：将空气引入DAC系统，通过风机或泵将其输送到吸附剂床层中。
2. **吸附**：空气中的二氧化碳与吸附剂发生化学反应或物理吸附，从而被分离出来。
3. **解吸**：将吸附剂加热或使用溶剂进行处理，使二氧化碳从吸附剂中释放出来。
4. **收集**：收集解吸出的二氧化碳，然后进行压缩、输送和储存。

**生物碳移除**：生物碳移除的核心算法涉及植物和微生物的碳固定过程。以下是生物碳移除的基本步骤：

1. **植物种植**：选择适合的碳捕获植物，如速生树木或藻类，进行大规模种植。
2. **光合作用**：植物通过光合作用吸收大气中的二氧化碳，并将其转化为有机物质。
3. **生物质转化**：将捕获的二氧化碳转化为生物质能或生物炭，用于能源生产或土壤改良。

**碳捕获和储存（CCS）**：CCS技术的核心算法涉及在工业排放源处捕捉二氧化碳，然后将其储存于地下岩层或其他储存设施中。以下是CCS技术的基本步骤：

1. **二氧化碳捕捉**：使用化学吸收剂或物理吸附剂从工业排放的烟气中捕捉二氧化碳。
2. **压缩和运输**：将捕捉到的二氧化碳压缩到高压状态，然后通过管道运输到储存地点。
3. **储存**：将二氧化碳注入地下岩层或其他储存设施中，确保其长期安全储存。

##### Core Algorithm Principles and Specific Operational Steps

##### 3.1 Core Algorithm Principles of Carbon Removal Technologies

**Direct Air Capture (DAC)**: The core algorithm of DAC technology involves the separation of carbon dioxide from the air using adsorbents or absorbents. Common adsorbents include amine compounds, organic solvents, and metal-organic frameworks (MOFs). Here are the basic steps of DAC technology:

1. **Air Inlet Processing**: Air is drawn into the DAC system through fans or pumps and directed to the adsorbent bed.
2. **Adsorption**: Carbon dioxide from the air reacts chemically or adsorbs physically to the adsorbent, thereby being separated.
3. **Desorption**: The adsorbent is heated or treated with a solvent to release the carbon dioxide.
4. **Collection**: The desorbed carbon dioxide is collected, compressed, transported, and stored.

3. **Biomass Conversion**: The captured carbon dioxide is converted into biomass energy or biochar for energy production or soil improvement.

**Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS)**: The core algorithm of CCS technology involves capturing carbon dioxide from industrial exhaust gases and then storing it in underground rock formations or other storage facilities. Here are the basic steps of CCS technology:

1. **Carbon Dioxide Capture**: Use chemical absorbents or physical adsorbents to capture carbon dioxide from flue gases emitted by industrial sources.
2. **Compression and Transportation**: Compress the captured carbon dioxide to a high pressure and transport it through pipelines to storage locations.
3. **Storage**: Inject the carbon dioxide into underground rock formations or other storage facilities to ensure long-term safe storage.

##### 4.1 直接空气捕捉（DAC）的数学模型

直接空气捕捉（DAC）技术中的关键数学模型涉及吸附剂的选择、吸附过程动力学和吸附剂再生过程。以下是一个简化的数学模型：

1. **吸附剂选择**：
   - 吸附容量（Q）：吸附剂单位质量能够吸附的二氧化碳量。
   - 吸附平衡常数（K）：描述吸附剂和二氧化碳之间的相互作用。

   吸附平衡可以用以下公式表示：
   $$ Q = K \cdot C_{\text{CO2}} $$

   其中，$C_{\text{CO2}}$ 是空气中的二氧化碳浓度。

2. **吸附过程动力学**：
   - 吸附速率（R）：描述吸附剂表面吸附二氧化碳的速度。
   - 吸附剂浓度（C）：吸附剂中二氧化碳的浓度。

   吸附速率可以用以下公式表示：
   $$ R = k \cdot C_{\text{CO2}} \cdot (1 - \frac{Q}{K}) $$

   其中，k 是吸附速率常数。

3. **吸附剂再生过程**：
   - 解吸速率（D）：描述吸附剂释放二氧化碳的速度。

   解吸速率可以用以下公式表示：
   $$ D = m \cdot \frac{dC}{dt} = k' \cdot C_{\text{CO2}} \cdot (1 - \frac{Q}{K}) $$

   其中，m 是解吸速率常数。

##### 4.2 生物碳移除的数学模型

生物碳移除过程中，关键数学模型涉及光合作用速率和生物质产量。以下是一个简化的数学模型：

1. **光合作用速率**：
   - 光合效率（Φ）：单位面积和单位时间吸收的二氧化碳量。
   - 光照强度（I）：单位面积接收到的太阳辐射量。

   光合作用速率可以用以下公式表示：
   $$ R_{\text{photosynthesis}} = \Phi \cdot I $$

2. **生物质产量**：
   - 生物量密度（ρ）：单位体积生物质的质量。
   - 生物质密度（B）：单位体积生物质中碳的质量。

   生物质产量可以用以下公式表示：
   $$ Y = \rho \cdot B \cdot R_{\text{photosynthesis}} $$

##### 4.3 碳捕获和储存（CCS）的数学模型

碳捕获和储存（CCS）过程中，关键数学模型涉及二氧化碳注入和地下储存。以下是一个简化的数学模型：

1. **二氧化碳注入速率**：
   - 注入速率（Q）：单位时间内注入的二氧化碳量。
   - 储存容量（V）：地下储存设施的总容量。

   二氧化碳注入速率可以用以下公式表示：
   $$ Q = \frac{C_{\text{in}} \cdot A}{V} $$

   其中，$C_{\text{in}}$ 是注入的二氧化碳浓度，A 是注入井的横截面积。

2. **地下储存容量**：
   - 储存效率（η）：单位体积地下岩层能够储存的二氧化碳量。

   其中，$V_{\text{rock}}$ 是地下岩层的总体积。

#### Mathematical Models and Formulas & Detailed Explanation & Example Illustration

##### 4.1 Mathematical Models of Direct Air Capture (DAC)

The key mathematical models in direct air capture (DAC) technology involve the selection of adsorbents, the dynamics of the adsorption process, and the regeneration process of the adsorbents. Here is a simplified mathematical model:

1. **Adsorbent Selection**:
   - Adsorption capacity (Q): The amount of carbon dioxide that an adsorbent can absorb per unit mass.
   - Adsorption equilibrium constant (K): Describes the interaction between the adsorbent and carbon dioxide.

   Adsorption equilibrium can be represented by the following equation:
   $$ Q = K \cdot C_{\text{CO2}} $$

   Where $C_{\text{CO2}}$ is the concentration of carbon dioxide in the air.

2. **Adsorption Process Dynamics**:
   - Adsorption rate (R): The speed at which carbon dioxide is adsorbed onto the surface of the adsorbent.
   - Adsorbent concentration (C): The concentration of carbon dioxide in the adsorbent.

   The adsorption rate can be represented by the following equation:
   $$ R = k \cdot C_{\text{CO2}} \cdot (1 - \frac{Q}{K}) $$

   Where k is the adsorption rate constant.

3. **Adsorbent Regeneration Process**:
   - Desorption rate (D): The speed at which carbon dioxide is released from the adsorbent.

   The desorption rate can be represented by the following equation:
   $$ D = m \cdot \frac{dC}{dt} = k' \cdot C_{\text{CO2}} \cdot (1 - \frac{Q}{K}) $$

   Where m is the desorption rate constant.

##### 4.2 Mathematical Models of Bio-carbon Removal

During the process of bio-carbon removal, key mathematical models involve the rate of photosynthesis and biomass production. Here is a simplified mathematical model:

1. **Photosynthesis Rate**:
   - Photosynthetic efficiency (Φ): The amount of carbon dioxide absorbed per unit area and unit time.
   - Light intensity (I): The amount of solar radiation received per unit area.

   The photosynthesis rate can be represented by the following equation:
   $$ R_{\text{photosynthesis}} = \Phi \cdot I $$

2. **Biomass Production**:
   - Biomass density (ρ): The mass of biomass per unit volume.
   - Carbon content (B): The mass of carbon in the biomass.

   The biomass production can be represented by the following equation:
   $$ Y = \rho \cdot B \cdot R_{\text{photosynthesis}} $$

##### 4.3 Mathematical Models of Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS)

During the process of carbon capture and storage (CCS), key mathematical models involve carbon dioxide injection and underground storage. Here is a simplified mathematical model:

1. **Carbon Dioxide Injection Rate**:
   - Injection rate (Q): The amount of carbon dioxide injected per unit time.
   - Storage capacity (V): The total capacity of the underground storage facility.

   The carbon dioxide injection rate can be represented by the following equation:
   $$ Q = \frac{C_{\text{in}} \cdot A}{V} $$

   Where $C_{\text{in}}$ is the concentration of injected carbon dioxide, and A is the cross-sectional area of the injection well.

2. **Underground Storage Capacity**:
   - Storage efficiency (η): The amount of carbon dioxide that can be stored per unit volume of underground rock formation.

   The underground storage capacity can be represented by the following equation:
   $$ V = \eta \cdot V_{\text{rock}} $$

   Where $V_{\text{rock}}$ is the total volume of the underground rock formation.

    """
    吸附过程的模拟

    Q: 吸附容量（kg/kg）
    t: 时间（秒）

    return adsorbed_CO2

    """
    解吸过程的模拟

    Q: 吸附容量（kg/kg）
    t: 时间（秒）
    """
    desorbed_CO2 = Q * (1 - np.exp(-m * C_CO2 * t))
    return desorbed_CO2

# 模拟吸附和解吸过程
C_CO2 = np.linspace(0.04, 0.08, 100)  # 空气中二氧化碳浓度
t = np.linspace(0, 3600, 100)  # 时间（秒）
