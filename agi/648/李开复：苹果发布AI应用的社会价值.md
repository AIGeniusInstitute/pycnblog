#### 4.1 机器学习中的线性回归模型

线性回归是一种简单的机器学习算法，用于预测连续值。其数学模型如下：

$$
y = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot x
$$

其中，$y$ 是目标变量，$x$ 是输入变量，$\beta_0$ 是截距，$\beta_1$ 是斜率。这个模型通过最小化误差平方和来拟合数据：

$$
\min \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - (\beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot x_i))^2
$$

例子：假设我们想要预测某个城市的平均温度（$y$）与当日最低温度（$x$）之间的关系。通过收集历史数据，我们得到以下数据点：

| 最低温度 (x) | 平均温度 (y) |
|--------------|--------------|
| 10           | 15           |
| 15           | 20           |
| 20           | 25           |

我们可以使用线性回归模型来拟合数据：

1. 计算斜率 $\beta_1$：
   $$
   \beta_1 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i - \bar{x})(y_i - \bar{y})}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i - \bar{x})^2}
   $$
   其中，$\bar{x}$ 和 $\bar{y}$ 分别是 $x$ 和 $y$ 的平均值。

2. 计算截距 $\beta_0$：
   $$
   \beta_0 = \bar{y} - \beta_1 \cdot \bar{x}
   $$

通过计算，我们得到 $\beta_1 = 0.5$，$\beta_0 = 10$。因此，线性回归模型为：

$$
y = 10 + 0.5 \cdot x
$$

使用这个模型，我们可以预测当最低温度为 20°C 时，平均温度为：

$$
y = 10 + 0.5 \cdot 20 = 15
$$

#### 4.2 机器学习中的逻辑回归模型

逻辑回归是一种用于分类问题的算法，其数学模型如下：

$$
P(y=1) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(\beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot x)}}
$$

其中，$P(y=1)$ 是目标变量为 1 的概率，$e$ 是自然对数的底。通过最大化似然函数来拟合数据：

$$
\max \ln L(\beta_0, \beta_1) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \ln \left[ \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(\beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot x_i)}} \right]^{y_i}
$$

例子：假设我们要分类邮件是否为垃圾邮件（$y=1$ 表示是垃圾邮件，$y=0$ 表示不是垃圾邮件），使用以下数据点：

| 邮件特征 (x) | 是否垃圾邮件 (y) |
|--------------|--------------|
| [0.1, 0.2]   | 1            |
| [0.3, 0.4]   | 0            |
| [0.5, 0.6]   | 1            |

我们可以使用逻辑回归模型来拟合数据：

1. 计算斜率 $\beta_1$：
   $$
   \beta_1 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - P(y=1)) \cdot x_i}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - P(y=1))}
   $$

2. 计算截距 $\beta_0$：
   $$
   \beta_0 = \bar{y} - \beta_1 \cdot \bar{x}
   $$

通过计算，我们得到 $\beta_1 = 0.2$，$\beta_0 = 0.1$。因此，逻辑回归模型为：

$$
P(y=1) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(0.1 + 0.2 \cdot x)}}
$$

使用这个模型，我们可以预测特征为 [0.2, 0.3] 的邮件是否为垃圾邮件：

$$
P(y=1) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(0.1 + 0.2 \cdot 0.2 + 0.3 \cdot 0.3)}} \approx 0.6
$$

概率接近 0.6，表明这是一个可能为垃圾邮件的邮件。

#### 4.3 深度学习中的卷积神经网络（CNN）

卷积神经网络（CNN）是一种用于图像识别和处理的深度学习算法。其核心思想是通过卷积层提取图像特征。以下是一个简化的CNN模型：

$$
h_{l}(x) = \sigma \left( \sum_{k} w_{lk} \cdot h_{l-1}(x) + b_{l} \right)
$$

其中，$h_{l}(x)$ 是第 $l$ 层的输出，$\sigma$ 是激活函数，$w_{lk}$ 是权重，$b_{l}$ 是偏置。

例子：假设我们有一个简单的CNN模型，包括两个卷积层和两个全连接层。输入图像为 $32 \times 32 \times 3$ 的彩色图像。

1. 第一个卷积层：
   $$
   h_{1}(x) = \sigma \left( \sum_{k} w_{1k} \cdot x + b_{1} \right)
   $$
   其中，$w_{1k}$ 和 $b_{1}$ 分别是权重和偏置。

2. 第一个池化层：
   $$
   h_{2}(x) = \max \left( \text{2D Max Pooling on } h_{1}(x) \right)
   $$

3. 第二个卷积层：
   $$
   h_{3}(x) = \sigma \left( \sum_{k} w_{3k} \cdot h_{2}(x) + b_{3} \right)
   $$

4. 第二个池化层：
   $$
   h_{4}(x) = \max \left( \text{2D Max Pooling on } h_{3}(x) \right)
   $$

5. 全连接层：
   $$
   h_{5}(x) = \sigma \left( \sum_{k} w_{5k} \cdot h_{4}(x) + b_{5} \right)
   $$

6. 输出层：
   $$
   y = \sigma \left( \sum_{k} w_{6k} \cdot h_{5}(x) + b_{6} \right)
   $$

通过这个模型，我们可以将图像分类到预定义的类别中。

#### 4.1 Linear Regression Model in Machine Learning

Linear regression is a simple machine learning algorithm used for predicting continuous values. Its mathematical model is as follows:

$$
y = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot x
$$

Where $y$ is the target variable, $x$ is the input variable, $\beta_0$ is the intercept, and $\beta_1$ is the slope. This model fits the data by minimizing the sum of squared errors:

$$
\min \sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - (\beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot x_i))^2
$$

Example: Suppose we want to predict the average temperature ($y$) of a city based on the daily minimum temperature ($x$). We have the following data points:

| Minimum Temperature (x) | Average Temperature (y) |
|------------------------|------------------------|
| 10                     | 15                     |
| 15                     | 20                     |
| 20                     | 25                     |

We can fit a linear regression model to the data:

1. Calculate the slope $\beta_1$:
   $$
   \beta_1 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i - \bar{x})(y_i - \bar{y})}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (x_i - \bar{x})^2}
   $$
   Where $\bar{x}$ and $\bar{y}$ are the averages of $x$ and $y$, respectively.

2. Calculate the intercept $\beta_0$:
   $$
   \beta_0 = \bar{y} - \beta_1 \cdot \bar{x}
   $$

After calculation, we get $\beta_1 = 0.5$ and $\beta_0 = 10$. Therefore, the linear regression model is:

$$
y = 10 + 0.5 \cdot x
$$

Using this model, we can predict the average temperature when the minimum temperature is 20°C:

$$
y = 10 + 0.5 \cdot 20 = 15
$$

#### 4.2 Logistic Regression Model in Machine Learning

Logistic regression is an algorithm used for classification problems. Its mathematical model is as follows:

$$
P(y=1) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(\beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot x)}}
$$

Where $P(y=1)$ is the probability that the target variable is 1, $e$ is the base of the natural logarithm. The model fits the data by maximizing the likelihood function:

$$
\max \ln L(\beta_0, \beta_1) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \ln \left[ \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(\beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot x_i)}} \right]^{y_i}
$$

Example: Suppose we want to classify emails as spam (where $y=1$ indicates spam and $y=0$ indicates not spam) using the following data points:

| Email Feature (x) | Is Spam (y) |
|-------------------|-------------|
| [0.1, 0.2]        | 1           |
| [0.3, 0.4]        | 0           |
| [0.5, 0.6]        | 1           |

We can fit a logistic regression model to the data:

1. Calculate the slope $\beta_1$:
   $$
   \beta_1 = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - P(y=1)) \cdot x_i}{\sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i - P(y=1))}
   $$

2. Calculate the intercept $\beta_0$:
   $$
   \beta_0 = \bar{y} - \beta_1 \cdot \bar{x}
   $$

After calculation, we get $\beta_1 = 0.2$ and $\beta_0 = 0.1$. Therefore, the logistic regression model is:

$$
P(y=1) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(0.1 + 0.2 \cdot x)}}
$$

Using this model, we can predict whether an email with features [0.2, 0.3] is spam:

$$
P(y=1) = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(0.1 + 0.2 \cdot 0.2 + 0.3 \cdot 0.3)}} \approx 0.6
$$

The probability is close to 0.6, indicating that this email is likely to be spam.

#### 4.3 Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) in Deep Learning

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) are a type of deep learning algorithm used for image recognition and processing. The core idea behind CNN is to extract image features through convolutional layers. Below is a simplified CNN model:

$$
h_{l}(x) = \sigma \left( \sum_{k} w_{lk} \cdot h_{l-1}(x) + b_{l} \right)
$$

Where $h_{l}(x)$ is the output of the $l$-th layer, $\sigma$ is the activation function, $w_{lk}$ is the weight, and $b_{l}$ is the bias.

Example: Suppose we have a simple CNN model with two convolutional layers and two fully connected layers. The input image is a $32 \times 32 \times 3$ color image.

1. First convolutional layer:
   $$
   h_{1}(x) = \sigma \left( \sum_{k} w_{1k} \cdot x + b_{1} \right)
   $$
   Where $w_{1k}$ and $b_{1}$ are weights and biases, respectively.

2. First pooling layer:
   $$
   h_{2}(x) = \max \left( \text{2D Max Pooling on } h_{1}(x) \right)
   $$

3. Second convolutional layer:
   $$
   h_{3}(x) = \sigma \left( \sum_{k} w_{3k} \cdot h_{2}(x) + b_{3} \right)
   $$

4. Second pooling layer:
   $$
   h_{4}(x) = \max \left( \text{2D Max Pooling on } h_{3}(x) \right)
   $$

5. Fully connected layer:
   $$
   h_{5}(x) = \sigma \left( \sum_{k} w_{5k} \cdot h_{4}(x) + b_{5} \right)
   $$

6. Output layer:
   $$
   y = \sigma \left( \sum_{k} w_{6k} \cdot h_{5}(x) + b_{6} \right)
   $$

Through this model, we can classify images into predefined categories.
